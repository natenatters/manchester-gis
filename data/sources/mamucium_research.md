# Mamucium Roman Fort Research Document

## Overview

**Mamucium** (also known as **Mancunium**) is a Roman auxiliary fort located in the Castlefield area of Manchester, England. It represents the origins of the city of Manchester and underwent three distinct construction phases:

1. **Wooden Fort** (AD 79) - Original turf and timber construction
2. **Stone Fort** (AD 200) - Stone-faced walls and gates
3. **Modern Reconstruction** (1984) - Partial reconstruction of north gate and ramparts

## Construction Phases

### Phase 1: Timber Fort (AD 79)

- **Date:** c. AD 79, under Governor Julius Agricola
- **Construction:** Turf and timber
- **Garrison:** ~500 auxiliary infantry (non-Roman citizens)
- **Defenses:**
  - Turf rampart with timber palisade
  - Double defensive ditches (V-shaped)
  - Standard width: ~10 Roman feet (2.96m)

### Phase 2: Expanded Fort (AD 160)

- **Date:** c. AD 160
- **Construction:** Still turf and timber
- **Changes:**
  - Fort nearly doubled in size
  - Expanded granaries (horrea)
  - Possible conversion to supply depot
- **Area:** 2 hectares

### Phase 3: Stone Fort (AD 200)

- **Date:** c. AD 200, possibly under Emperor Septimius Severus
- **Construction:**
  - Stone-faced gatehouses
  - Stone cladding over turf ramparts
  - Wall thickness: 2.1-2.7 metres
- **Features:**
  - Rebuilt porta principalis (main gates)
  - Enhanced defensive ditches

### Decline (3rd-4th Century)

- Vicus abandoned by mid-3rd century
- Small garrison may have remained until c. AD 400
- North gate blocked up, causeway removed

## Vicus (Civilian Settlement)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Location | West, north, and east of fort |
| Area | ~26 hectares |
| Peak Population | ~2,000 inhabitants |
| Period | 2nd-3rd century AD |

### Known Structures

1. **Mansio** (340 Deansgate)
   - Two-storey Roman hotel for officials
   - Colonnade/veranda enclosure
   - On cursus publicus route

2. **Romano-Celtic Temple**
   - Wooden structure with sandstone wall
   - Finds: cremated remains, samian pottery, lead curse figurine
   - Demolished mid-3rd century

3. **Industrial Complex**
   - "Early industrial estate" with furnaces
   - Large-scale iron production
   - Pottery kilns, metal furnaces, tanning pits

4. **Bathhouse** (location recorded but details limited)

5. **Residential Buildings**
   - Wood construction
   - Wattle and daub walls
   - Thatched or tiled roofs

## Modern Reconstruction (1984)

### What's Visible Today

1. **North Gate Reconstruction**
   - Built on original foundations
   - Incorporates original Roman stonework
   - Shows AD 200 appearance

2. **Ramparts**
   - Walkable reconstructed sections
   - Original ditch system visible

3. **Mural Wall**
   - Shows different construction phases

4. **Vicus Building Foundations**
   - Three excavated buildings displayed
   - In-situ preservation

5. **Granary Remains**
   - Excavated foundations visible

## Typical Roman Fort Construction (for 3D Modeling Reference)

### Rampart Specifications

| Element | Dimension |
|---------|-----------|
| Height | ~10 feet (3m) |
| Base Width | 12-16 feet (3.6-4.9m) |
| Top Width | 4-10 feet (1.2-3m) |
| Turf Wall Thickness | ~1 yard (0.9m) each side |
| Core Gap | ~10 feet (3m) |

### Gate Construction

- **Porta Praetoria:** Main front gate (facing enemy)
- **Porta Decumana:** Rear gate
- **Portae Principales:** Side gates (left/right)
- **Features:**
  - Double arched entrances
  - Wooden doors (possibly metal-plated)
  - 2-3 storey towers
  - Separate protective ditches

### Internal Roads

- **Via Praetoria:** Front axis
- **Via Decumana:** Rear axis
- **Via Principalis:** Cross-road
- **Via Quintana:** Secondary cross-road
- **Via Sagularis:** Perimeter road

### Standard Buildings

- **Principia:** Headquarters (central)
- **Praetorium:** Commander's residence
- **Horrea:** Granaries (often on Via Principalis)
- **Valetudinarium:** Hospital
- **Fabricae:** Workshops
- **Barracks:** 30-100m x 7-15m, 10-13 contubernia each
